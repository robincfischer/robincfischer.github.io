About

I am a first-year PhD researcher at the Institute of Data, Energy, and Sustainability in Mathematics at the Vienna University of Economics and Business, supervised by Kavita Surana. My research centres on leveraging Bayesian inference and machine-learning methods to analyse and predict the adaptation dynamics of power firms.

Previously, I obtained an M.Sc. in Economics from the University of Cologne and Durham University and a B.A. in Mathematics & Philosophy from TU Braunschweig, where I was a scholar of the German Academic Scholarship Foundation.

My broader academic interests lie in the areas of complex networks, climate change AI, technological forecasting, and Bayesian econometrics. Outside academia I have worked as a data analyst at Daimler AG. When I am not coding in Python or R, I mentor first-generation students and enjoy tennis.
