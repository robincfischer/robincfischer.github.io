About Me

I am a PhD researcher at IDEaS, where I apply forecasting and control methods to enhance energy system models, with a particular focus on innovation and technological change. My research lies at the intersection of energy economics, complex systems, and statistical modeling, aiming to understand the dynamics of technological transitions in the energy sector. I am advised by Prof. Kavita Surana.

Research Interests

My research explores the interplay between technological change, uncertainty, and policy incentives in energy transitions. Specifically, I work on:

  • Energy System Forecasting: Leveraging Bayesian inference and machine learning to analyze and predict the evolution of firm portfolio dynamics in changing energy markets.
  • Technological Change & Innovation: Studying Wright’s Law and experience curves to assess the evolution of renewable energy costs and diffusion patterns.
  • Complex Systems in Energy Economics: Using agent-based modeling (ABM) and network analysis to explore the nonlinear dynamics of energy transitions.
  • Econometrics & Economic History: Investigating the historical role of policy interventions, market incentives, and technological lock-ins in shaping the energy landscape.

Brief Bio

I hold a MSc in Economics and a Bsc in Mathematics and Philosophy. My research integrates quantitative techniques from econometrics, complexity science, and control theory to better understand the economic and technological forces driving the energy transition.
