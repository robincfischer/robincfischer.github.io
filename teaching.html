Teaching

When I think back to my own time as a student, I remember the moments when certain concepts just wouldn’t click—when I stared at a problem set feeling completely lost, or when I read the same paragraph three times without understanding a word. I also remember the professors and teaching assistants who helped make those difficult moments easier. That experience shaped my approach to teaching. I don’t believe in trick questions or exams that are designed to make students fail. I believe in fair but challenging exams, with clear expectations set in advance so students know what it takes to pass. I care deeply about educational equity. I believe that for many students one of the biggest challenges is not the subject matter itself, but learning the academic habitus: the implicit rules and expectations that shape university life. Below, I’ve compiled a set of resources I find valuable for making this academic culture more transparent and accessible.
