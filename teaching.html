Teaching

When I think back to my own time as a student, I remember the moments when certain concepts just wouldn’t click—when I stared at a problem set feeling completely lost, or when I read the same paragraph three times without understanding a word. I also remember the professors and teaching assistants who helped make those difficult moments easier. That experience shaped my approach to teaching. I don’t believe in trick questions or exams that are designed to make students fail. I believe in fair but challenging assessments—exams and exercises that push students to think critically and apply their knowledge, but that are also transparent and well-structured. I know that everyone learns differently. Some people need visual explanations, others prefer step-by-step logical breakdowns, and some absorb knowledge best by listening. That’s why I try to provide a variety of materials—textbooks, diagrams, structured notes, and audio/video resources—so that every student has the tools they need to succeed.
